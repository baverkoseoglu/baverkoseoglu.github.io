About

Baver Köseoğlu — Architecture Student

Architecture as a practice of empathy and territory.

I am an architecture student with a particular interest in the spaces where cities become fragile — and the infrastructures that hold them together. My work oscillates between the rigors of structural thinking and the quieter questions of how people inhabit their environments.

I am drawn to disaster architecture not as spectacle, but as necessity — a field where design decisions have direct consequences on human dignity. Alongside studio work, I conduct research into spatial behavior, urban resilience, and the social dimensions of building.

My approach favors clarity over complexity: spaces that are honest about what they are, built for the people who will actually use them.
